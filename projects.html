2025

Academic Youth Empowerment

In February 2025, Wezesha Youth Initiative hosted an educational outreach event at Maono High School in Kibra, Nairobi, aimed at addressing the challenges faced by youth in slum communities. The event focused on equipping students with knowledge and tools to overcome barriers such as unemployment, limited access to education, mental health stigma, and substance abuse. It featured interactive sessions on leadership, financial literacy, and youth advocacy.

The event was organized by Wezesha Youth Initiative in collaboration with Maono Education Centre and local community leaders. It brought together students, teachers, and youth mentors to create a space for open dialogue and practical learning. The discussions targeted core issues affecting the youth, with the goal of inspiring action and fostering resilience among participants.

The outreach also highlighted the importance of community-driven solutions, including mentorship, scholarship opportunities, and mental health support. The sessions empowered participants to become agents of change within their own neighborhoods.

Overall, the 2025 education outreach at Maono High School was a meaningful step in strengthening youth capacity in Kibra. The event not only raised awareness but also offered actionable strategies that continue to inspire and impact young lives in the community.

International Day of the Women

March 8, 2024 — In the heart of Kibra, where resilience meets hope, Wezesha Youth Initiative marked the International Day of the Woman with a powerful celebration dedicated to honoring, uplifting, and empowering women in the community. The event brought together young women, mothers, youth leaders, and community partners under the theme: "Dignity, Opportunity, and Empowerment for Every Woman."

The day was more than just a celebration—it was a call to action. Recognizing the unique challenges faced by women and girls in informal settlements, Wezesha created a space for learning, sharing, and support. The event opened with heartfelt words from the chairperson, who emphasized the organization’s mission: to empower the youth of Kibra—especially young women—through education, mentorship, and community-led programs.

The success of the event was made possible through the generous support of partners, well-wishers, and Wezesha members who contributed financially and volunteered their time. Each contribution, no matter how small, became part of a greater impact—one that continues beyond the day itself.

Wezesha Youth Initiative remains committed to its vision of empowering youth—one life at a time. Celebrating women is not confined to one day, but woven into the fabric of Wezesha’s mission throughout the year. As we reflect on this year’s International Women’s Day, one thing is clear: when women rise, communities rise. And in Kibra, Wezesha Youth Initiative is helping lead that rise, one empowered woman at a time.

About

History

Wezesha Youth Initiative was born out of a deep need to respond to the challenges faced by young people in Kibra, one of the largest informal settlements in Nairobi. What began as a small group of passionate youth leaders coming together to create change has grown into a vibrant, community-based organization committed to transforming lives. The name “Wezesha,” which means “to enable” in Swahili, reflects the heart of the initiative—to create opportunities where there were none, and to give hope where it had been lost. The organization was established with a clear purpose: to empower young people, especially those who had dropped out of school due to poverty, early pregnancies, lack of mentorship, or other social barriers. From its early days, Wezesha focused on practical solutions such as school reintegration programs for teenage mothers, scholarship sourcing for vulnerable students, mental health awareness, financial literacy training, and skill-building workshops. Over the years, the initiative has become a trusted pillar in the community, working hand-in-hand with schools, parents, local leaders, and volunteers to ensure no youth is left behind.
