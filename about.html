About

History

Wezesha Youth Initiative was born out of a deep need to respond to the challenges faced by young people in Kibra, one of the largest informal settlements in Nairobi. What began as a small group of passionate youth leaders coming together to create change has grown into a vibrant, community-based organization committed to transforming lives.

The name “Wezesha,” which means “to enable” in Swahili, reflects the heart of the initiative—to create opportunities where there were none, and to give hope where it had been lost. The organization was established with a clear purpose: to empower young people, especially those who had dropped out of school due to poverty, early pregnancies, lack of mentorship, or other social barriers. From its early days, Wezesha focused on practical solutions such as school reintegration programs for teenage mothers, scholarship sourcing for vulnerable students, mental health awareness, financial literacy training, and skill-building workshops.

Over the years, the initiative has become a trusted pillar in the community, working hand-in-hand with schools, parents, local leaders, and volunteers to ensure no youth is left behind. Its commitment to long-term impact continues to inspire hope and transformation, as Wezesha empowers a new generation to rise, lead, and build a better future—not just for themselves, but for their entire community.

Our Vision

To build a future where every young person regardless of circumstance, has the opportunity to thrive through education, empowerment, and holistic support. We envision a society where teenage mothers are rehabilitated and empowered to reclaim their dreams through schooling, where both boys and girls access quality education through scholarships, and where guidance, counseling, and youth development initiatives inspire confident, capable, and responsible generations..

Our work

Our Mission

To transform the lives of vulnerable youth by rehabilitating and reintegrating teenage mothers into the education system, sourcing scholarships to ensure equal access to quality education for both boys and girls, providing holistic guidance and counseling services, and empowering young people with the skills, knowledge, and opportunities they need to build a brighter future.
